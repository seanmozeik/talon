# Layout Sketches

Three spatial concepts for tasting counter integration into Calle Sur dining room. Each balances kitchen workflow, guest experience, and buildout budget. No final rendering yet—sketches are conceptual, inform contractor conversations.

## Option A: Linear Bar (West Wall)

**Concept:** 12 ft counter along west wall of dining room. Guests face kitchen. Kitchen prep station behind/below counter in existing galley.

**Pros:**
- Lowest structural cost (no new wall, no complex exhaust routing)
- Guests see plating action (theater)
- Minimizes main dining footprint disruption
- Counter is linear, clean setup

**Cons:**
- Exhaust must route through existing hood (capacity risk if hood is at limit)
- Main dining room feels more compact (lose ~80 sqft to counter zone)
- Limited sightlines from counter to main kitchen (separate prep means slower handoff)

**Layout:** Counter 12 ft × 3 ft depth, 8 stools. Backrest prevents spillover to kitchen. Plumbing/electrical runs easily along west wall.

---

## Option B: U-Shape (Northeast Corner)

**Concept:** Counter wraps 90° in northeast corner. 6 ft north wall + 6 ft east wall. Creates intimate chef's counter within main room.

**Pros:**
- Separates tasting from main dining visually (better experience isolation)
- Chef/Lúcia controls both kitchen + counter plating (tighter workflow)
- Kitchen prep station is immediately behind (fast handoffs)
- Theater feeling is higher (three-sided sightlines)

**Cons:**
- Requires moving 4–6 main dining tables (capacity loss: ~10% seating)
- More complex plumbing/electrical routing (corner utility trench)
- Exhaust needs dedicated duct (new hood or duct extension to existing hood: $3K–$5K extra)
- Construction is 2–3 weeks (vs. 1 week for Option A)

**Layout:** 6 ft × 3 ft on north, 6 ft × 3 ft on east, corner seat elevated slightly for sightlines. Two-chef setup feasible (main line cook + tasting cook).

---

## Option C: Hybrid (Island Center)

**Concept:** Freestanding 10 ft × 4 ft island, offset center-stage in dining room. 6 seats on one long side, 2 on ends.

**Pros:**
- Maximum theater (surrounded by guests, open sightlines)
- Kitchen workflow unchanged (tasting counter is pseudo-independent)
- Visually striking, memorable guest experience

**Cons:**
- **Major structural cost:** New island requires dedicated exhaust (vertical duct, makeup air, $8K–$12K)
- Plumbing: two new lines (cost: $1.5K–$2K)
- Main dining room becomes cramped (lose ~120 sqft, main capacity drops to 32 seats)
- Most disruptive to current operations during build-out

**Layout:** Island is center-stage, 10 ft long, 4 ft depth. Prep bench on one side (visible), plating on other. Requires new 8" exhaust duct to roof (significant work).

---

## Recommendation

**Go with Option A (Linear Bar).** Reasons:
1. **Budget:** Lowest cost (~$27K–$29K build-out). Build confidence before committing to $35K+.
2. **Risk:** Existing infrastructure (exhaust, plumbing) handles linear wall setup. No unknown variables.
3. **Workflow:** Lúcia manages both kitchen + counter plating in tight space (tight is fine, proven in small kitchens globally).
4. **Timeline:** 1 week build. Landlord approval likely faster (minimal structural impact).
5. **Pivot:** If soft opening (Aug 2026) succeeds, upgrade to Option B (U-shape) in winter 2026–27 with clearer data on demand/ROI.

Option A gets us to market fastest. Option B is the long-term vision.
