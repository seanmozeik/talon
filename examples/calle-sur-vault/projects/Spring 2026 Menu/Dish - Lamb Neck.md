# Lamb Neck

**Price:** $34 | **Plate cost:** ~$9.50 | **Margin:** 3.6x

## Concept

Slow-braised lamb neck, served warm with intensity. Earthy, collagen-rich, the showstopper protein. Plating is reductive: meat, sauce, single vegetable hero (spring garlic, roasted radish, or young carrot depending on week).

Inspired by [[Recipe Clip - Smoked Lamb Neck]]. Method is low-and-slow braise, not smoking, adapted for kitchen constraints.

## Components

- Lamb neck (Costera Fish or local distributor), 7–8 oz per plate
- Lamb or veal stock (see [[Stock Theory]])
- Red wine (dry, rustic, ~$18/bottle)
- Aromatics: carrot, celery, onion, bay, thyme
- Garnish vegetables: spring garlic, radish, baby carrot (seasonal rotation)
- Fleur de sel, black pepper

## Technique: Braising

1. **Sear:** Season neck pieces (175g each, cut into 2–3 pieces per service portion). Sear on high heat in heavy pot, 3 min per side, get color.
2. **Aromatics:** Remove meat, sauté carrot, celery, onion until golden, 5 min.
3. **Deglaze:** Red wine, scrape bottom, reduce by 50%, ~3 min.
4. **Braise:** Return meat, add stock to 75% submerge. Bay, thyme, cracked black pepper. Bring to gentle simmer.
5. **Oven time:** 180°C, covered, 3 hours until fork-tender. Skim fat every 45 min.
6. **Sauce:** Strain braising liquid, skim fat, reduce until glossy (1.5 cups stock → ~0.75 cups sauce). Season.

**Make-ahead:** Braise 1 day before service, cool in braising liquid, reheat gently on service (gentler texture). Sauce can be prepped 2 days ahead.

## Service Plating

- **Plate:** 11" white, warm.
- **Sauce:** Spoon 60 ml glossy sauce in gentle curve across bottom.
- **Meat:** Nest 140g (cooked wt) lamb neck, top of sauce. Intact chunks, not shredded.
- **Vegetable:** One roasted spring garlic bulb or 3 roasted radish wedges, placed north.
- **Finish:** Fleur de sel, turn of black pepper mill, 0.5 tsp olive oil if plate reads dry.

See [[Plating Composition]] for approach.

## Sourcing

- Lamb neck: [[Costera Fish]] (Renato), confirm 2–3 weeks ahead for volume. Expect 8–10 necks/week for 40 covers.
- Stock: in-house (see [[Stock Theory]]), or buy from Costera if in-house volume insufficient.
- Red wine: house wine list (David to choose bottle suitable for both drinking and cooking, 2023–2024 vintage).
- Spring garlic/radish/carrot: [[Salt Marsh Spring 2026]] or [[Foraging Program]] foraged sources.

## Refinement Notes

First braise test (March 2026) was mushy—overcooked at 3.5 hours. Revised to 3 hours, tested April 18 (tasting team loved it). Texture now perfect: yielding but with bite. Sauce reduction is critical—too thin and it doesn't coat, too thick and it's muddy. Aim for light gloss, 1 mm coating on spoon.

## Storage

Braised meat holds 3 days in braising liquid at 4°C. Sauce holds 4 days. Reheats well; never boil—warm gently at 60°C.
