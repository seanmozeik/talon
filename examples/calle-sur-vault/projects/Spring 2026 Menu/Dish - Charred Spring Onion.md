# Charred Spring Onion

**Price:** $18 | **Plate cost:** ~$4.20 | **Margin:** 4.3x

## Concept

Whole spring onion, buttered and charred hard under salamander until blackened on cut face. Whey cream (from [[Fava and Whey]]) as base, micro garlic shoots, fleur de sel, olive oil.

**Why it works:** Vegetable-forward simplicity. [[Maillard Reaction]] doing the heavy lifting—char flavor pulls against cool, acidic whey. No protein distraction.

## Components

- Spring onion (Salt Marsh), 2 per plate, cleaned
- Whey cream: liquid whey from fava fermentation, reduced 40%, finished butter + salt
- Garlic shoots (wild, foraged or from suppliers)
- Fleur de sel
- Extra virgin olive oil (Arbequina, Spanish)

## Technique

1. **Prep:** Split onion lengthwise, keep root intact. Brush butter + salt into cut face.
2. **Char:** Salamander blast until cut side is blackened, ~3 min. White part stays just tender.
3. **Plate:** Spoon whey cream in offset arc. Nest charred onion, root up. Scatter garlic shoots, fleur de sel, 0.5 tsp olive oil drizzle.

**Timing:** 4 min active. Whey cream can be prepped 2 days ahead.

## Plating

Offset, asymmetric. See [[Plating Composition]] for philosophy. White plate (11"), cream pool points northeast. Onion sits north-central, blackened face catches light. Shoots/salt on south side. The char texture is the image.

## Allergens

- Dairy (cream, butter)

## Refinement Notes

First pass (April 18 tasting) tasted muddy—whey was too neutral, killing char brightness. Revision: reduce whey further, boost olive oil by 0.25 tsp, add wild garlic shoots instead of cultivated. Retasted April 22 internally (staff meal)—Lúcia confirmed it's alive now.
