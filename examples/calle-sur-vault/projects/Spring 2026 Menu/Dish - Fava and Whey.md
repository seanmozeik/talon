# Fava and Whey

**Price:** $16 | **Plate cost:** ~$3.80 | **Margin:** 4.2x

## Concept

Lacto-fermented fava bean purée, served warm with whey broth and crisp fava tendrils. Inspired by [[Recipe Clip - Fava Whey Soup]]. No cream—whey does the acidity and lightness. Acidic, alive, vegetable-first.

## Components

- Fava beans (Salt Marsh, fresh or frozen), shelled
- Salt for fermentation (~2.5% by weight)
- Fava tendrils (fresh, spring harvest)
- Whey broth: clarified whey from fermentation, chicken stock reduction
- Olive oil (Arbequina)
- Kosher salt, micro mint (optional)

## Technique: Fermentation

1. **Blanch & cool:** Fresh fava beans blanched 2 min, shocked, peeled (tedious, non-negotiable).
2. **Ferment:** Mix peeled beans + 2.5% kosher salt by weight, pack jar, weight to submerge, cloth cover.
3. **Ferment time:** 5–7 days at 18–20°C. Target: tangy, slightly soft, not mushy. Taste daily after day 4.

**Make-ahead:** Ferment 10 days before service window.

## Service

1. **Purée:** Fermented fava + 30% their weight in whey broth, blender until velvety. Season to taste (usually just salt check—fermentation is salty enough).
2. **Plate:** Spoon warm (reheated gently) into 9" bowl center. Pour 40 ml whey broth around. Top with 4–5 crisp tendrils, 0.25 tsp olive oil, fleck of sea salt.

**Timing:** 6 min active (reheat + plate).

## Plating

Centered in shallow bowl, cream-colored purée, dark green tendrils scattered across. Whey should be transparent, not opaque. Minimalist. Visual weight is simplicity.

## Allergens

- None (unless stock used; clarify with David/Renata on any shellfish stock prep)

## Wine Pairing

David's recommendation: **Sauvignon Blanc** (Loire or Sancerre-style, 2023–2024 vintage) or **Skin-contact white** (natural, funky acid cuts the ferment perfectly). Pairs because fermented acidity meets wine acidity—clean, alive.

## Sourcing

- Fava beans: [[Salt Marsh Spring 2026]] (peak April–May)
- Tendrils: foraged or bought from Costera supplementary order
- Whey: produced during fermentation or buy liquid whey from [[Vega Dairy]] (Andrés) if volume demand spikes

## Refinement Notes

Recipe adapted from [[Recipe Clip - Fava Whey Soup]] (NYT source). First test batch (March 2026) was slightly over-fermented (8 days)—too sour. Reduced to 5–6 days, hit sweet spot. Tendrils are textural hero; don't skip them.
