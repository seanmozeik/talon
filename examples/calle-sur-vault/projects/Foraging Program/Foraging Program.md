# Foraging Program

Quarterly foraging walks with Maria (Salt Marsh Farm) to source wild & semi-wild ingredients for seasonal menus. Supplements cultivated sourcing; drives menu innovation. Spring 2026 walk was April 12; fall walk next (target late August).

## Vision

Wild ingredients are Calle Sur's secret weapon: marsh greens, ramps, fiddleheads, wild fennel, foraged mushrooms. Margin benefit (foraged = low cost, high perceived value) + narrative power ("hand-foraged by Maria"). Vegetable-forward cooking demands these textures & flavors.

Maria knows the marshland intimately (30+ years). Her inventory is dynamic, unpredictable by outsiders. Worth trusting her curated selection rather than dictating demand.

## Annual Cycle

### Spring (March–May)

Ramps, fiddleheads, marsh greens, wild fennel pollen, watercress, chickweed, nettles.

**Walk:** April 12, 2026 (completed)  
**Haul:** See [[Salt Marsh Spring 2026]] for inventory.  
**Usage:** Charred Spring Onion (garnish), Fava and Whey (tendrils), Lamb Neck (garlic shoots), rotational specials.

### Summer (June–August)

Wild berries (blackberry, raspberry), late fennel, purslane, wild sorrel, early mushrooms (chanterelles by August).

**Walk:** July 15 (tentative, invite Lúcia + David)  
**Expected haul:** ~30–40 kg foraged items, heavily berries.  
**Usage:** Desserts (Ana), salad components, garnishes. Fresh or preserved (drying, fermentation).

### Fall (September–October)

Mushrooms (chanterelles, porcini, hedgehogs), late berries, wild leeks, sorrel, edible seeds.

**Walk:** August 20 (target, before September menu lock)  
**Expected haul:** Mushrooms are the gold. 20–30 kg if productive year.

### Winter (November–February)

Dormant forage, but preserved items (dried mushrooms, fermented greens, salted wild leeks) sustain menu interest. No active walk; use inventory built in fall.

## Logistics & Cost

**Cost model:**
- Maria charges $30/kg for foraged items (reasonable rate for her labor + expertise)
- Typical haul: 20–40 kg/walk → $600–$1,200 per seasonal walk
- Usage scope: 3–4 dishes + specials per quarter
- Margin: foraged items cost $30/kg, sell (as component) in $18–$34 plates → strong margin

**Quality assurance:**
- Maria provides species ID + harvest location notes (traceability, in case health dept asks)
- Inspection on delivery: no bruised/wilted items. Return any sub-grade.
- Storage: refrigerate immediately. Use within 3 days for peak freshness.

**Seasonal prep:**
- Spring haul (April): Use immediately over 2–3 weeks. Dry excess (fennel pollen), ferment extras (ramps → kimchi).
- Summer haul (July): Preserve heavily. Dry mushrooms, freeze berries (for winter use).
- Fall haul (Aug/Sept): Dry everything possible. Mushroom season yields dried goods for winter storage.

## Risk & Notes

- **Availability:** Wild ingredients are unpredictable. Late frost = no ramps. Dry summer = fewer mushrooms. Menu must be flexible (foraging supplements, not replaces, cultivated sourcing).
- **Health/Safety:** Maria is responsible for species ID and harvesting. I trust her judgment completely. But inventory sourcing notes on all hauls (legal/traceability).
- **Narrative:** Guests love the story ("hand-foraged that morning"). Use it. Describe dishes as "foraged ramp" or "wild fennel" on menu cards.
