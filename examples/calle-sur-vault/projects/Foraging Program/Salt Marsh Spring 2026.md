# Salt Marsh Spring 2026

Foraged inventory from April 12 walk with Maria (Salt Marsh Farm). Detailed usage plan & sourcing notes.

## Inventory Haul

**Date:** April 12, 2026  
**Participants:** Marco, Lúcia, Renata, Maria  
**Location:** Salt Marsh tidal zones, 5 miles north of Cala  
**Total weight:** ~12 kg foraged items  
**Cost:** $360 (12 kg @ $30/kg)

---

## Items & Usage

### Ramps (4 kg)

**Description:** Wild leeks, Allium tricoccum. Bulb + green stem both edible. Peppery, onion-forward.

**Current status:** 2 kg used (fresh), 2 kg preserved.

**Usage plan:**
- [[Charred Spring Onion]] — garlic shoots garnish (0.8 kg, used)
- Fermented hot sauce testing (see [[Recipe v3]]) — 0.5 kg fresh ramps in v1 test batch (Jan 2026)
- Ramp butter for fish specials (Lúcia to make, freeze 1 kg)
- Dried ramp powder for winter seasoning (Anna prepping, ~100g dry yield)

**Storage:** Refrigerated, stems wrapped in damp cloth. Will hold 1 week; use by April 19 for peak freshness.

---

### Fiddleheads (2 kg)

**Description:** Ostrich fern fronds, tightly coiled. Nutty, tender when cooked. Short season (April only).

**Current status:** 1.5 kg used, 0.5 kg preserved.

**Usage plan:**
- Sautéed with butter + sea salt, side dish for Lamb Neck specials (Lúcia prepped April 13, tasted excellent)
- Fermented: 0.5 kg in lacto brine (2.5% salt, 5-day ferment) for winter use
- May rotate into rotational menu slot if David approves pairing

**Storage:** Cool, damp. Use within 5 days; after that, texture deteriorates.

---

### Marsh Greens (3 kg)

**Description:** Mixed wild lettuce species (Lactuca, Chenopodium?), identified by Maria. Tender, mild bitterness, herbaceous.

**Current status:** 2 kg used fresh, 1 kg drying.

**Usage plan:**
- Salad/garnish for upcoming spring dishes (paired with Fava and Whey on tasting)
- Dried for winter tea or broth component (Ana experimenting with dried leaf infusions)
- Preserve 0.5 kg by drying (hang-dried in cool room, target: 50g dry yield)

**Storage:** Refrigerated, damp paper towel wrapped. Peak: 3 days. Wilts quickly.

---

### Wild Fennel Pollen (0.5 kg)

**Description:** Flowering fennel fronds, dried then sifted for pollen. Bright, anise-forward, potent.

**Current status:** All reserved for Ana (pastry).

**Usage plan:**
- Spring dessert components (panna cotta infusion? pastry dust?)
- Ana to develop recipes by May 15 for dessert menu finalization

**Storage:** Airtight jar, cool dark place. Shelf life: 12 months.

**Note:** Fennel pollen is expensive to buy retail ($25–$40/oz). This haul (0.5 kg) is worth ~$200 if purchased. High-value acquisition.

---

### Watercress (1.5 kg)

**Description:** Nasturtium officinale, grows in cool springs. Peppery, crisp, very fresh-tasting.

**Current status:** 1 kg used, 0.5 kg preserved.

**Usage plan:**
- Fresh in spring salads (specials)
- Watercress oil (blended with oil, then strained) for fish plates
- 0.5 kg blanched + frozen for winter stock/soup base

**Storage:** Standing in water (like flowers) keeps 2 weeks. Alternatively, refrigerated: 5 days.

---

### Garlic Shoots (0.8 kg)

**Description:** Young garlic flower stems, pencil-thin. Mild garlic bite, tender, delicate.

**Current status:** All used.

**Usage plan:**
- [[Charred Spring Onion]] (revised, April 24) — 0.8 kg scattered as micro garnish (0.25 tsp per plate, ~8g per cover)
- Testing showed that wild garlic shoots (vs. cultivated chives) elevate dish from "pretty" to "alive"

**Storage:** Refrigerated, wrapped. Use within 3 days for best texture.

## Seasonal Context

Spring forage is peak season (cool, wet conditions favor tender growth). Summer will shift to berries & mushrooms. This haul is representative of April–May abundance.

Quality of forage drives menu creativity. Lúcia & team are energized by these ingredients—they unlock dishes that cultivated sourcing alone can't.
