# Co-Packer Research

Evaluated 3 co-packing facilities for fermented hot sauce production. Goal: find partner with fermentation expertise, regulatory compliance, and capacity for 2,400 bottles/month (ramp to 5,000+ year 2).

## Vendor Summary

### 1. **Artisan Ferments Co** (Regional, CA-based)

**Capacity:** 50L fermentation + bottling line  
**Specialization:** Lacto-fermented products (kimchi, hot sauce, koji cultures)  
**Cost:** $1.20 COGS (all-in: fermentation, bottling, label)  
**Lead time:** 4 weeks (first batch), then 2-week turnaround per batch  
**Regulatory:** FDA 21 CFR Part 117 compliant, HACCP plan on file  

**Strengths:**
- Deep fermentation expertise (they know 20°C temp control intimately)
- Small-batch mindset (not industrial, respects craft angle)
- Transparent traceability (batch logs, testing photos shared)
- Flexible SKU rotation (easy to shift Fresno → aji limo mid-season)

**Concerns:**
- Slightly higher COGS than competitors ($0.10/unit premium)
- Owned by single operator (no backup if illness/burnout)
- Facility is small garage-adjacent (not insurance-guaranteed, risk assessment needed)

**Quote example:** 3,000 bottles Classic Red (5 batches, 600 bottles each) @ $1.20 = $3,600. Includes recipe development, ferment monitoring, bottling, boxes, labels.

---

### 2. **Valley Food Systems** (Large, Central CA)

**Capacity:** 500L fermentation (shared facility) + high-speed bottling  
**Specialization:** Hot sauces, salsas, pepper products (general condiments, not specialty ferments)  
**Cost:** $0.95 COGS  
**Lead time:** 6 weeks (first batch due to scheduling), 1-week turnaround thereafter  
**Regulatory:** FDA approved, USDA facility certified

**Strengths:**
- Lowest COGS ($0.95 vs $1.20 vs $1.30)
- Industrial-scale capacity (can ramp to 10,000 bottles/month if needed)
- Established distribution connections (suggestion: regional retail partners they work with)
- Bottling automation = consistent fill weights

**Concerns:**
- Shared fermentation vat with other clients (flavor cross-contamination risk if not careful)
- Process-focused, not craft-focused (may view ferment as commodity vinegar-pickle process)
- Less transparency on fermentation logs (not typical for large co-packers)
- Minimum order: 3,000 bottles per batch (inflexible if sales are slower than projected)

**Quote example:** 5,000 bottles Classic Red (2 batches) @ $0.95 = $4,750. Does NOT include label design or packaging; assumes you provide artwork.

---

### 3. **Coastal Artisan Foods** (Local, Cala-based)

**Capacity:** 30L fermentation + semi-manual bottling  
**Specialization:** Hot sauces, fermented condiments, small-batch (6 months in business)  
**Cost:** $1.30 COGS  
**Lead time:** 3 weeks (first batch), 10 days thereafter  
**Regulatory:** FDA permitted (new facility, first-year oversight phase)

**Strengths:**
- Local (10 min drive from Calle Sur, easy oversight)
- Newest facility, cleanest equipment
- Very flexible batch sizing (can do 500-bottle batches if needed for testing)
- Owner understands Calle Sur brand (local credibility)

**Concerns:**
- **Highest COGS** ($0.15 premium vs Artisan Ferments)
- **First-year facility risk:** FDA oversight ongoing, could face compliance surprises
- Limited track record (opened Fall 2025; only 3 clients to date)
- Bottling is semi-manual (less consistent than industrial, more quality variance risk)
- If owner burns out, facility may close (startup risk)

**Quote example:** 2,000 bottles Classic Red @ $1.30 = $2,600. Includes everything (ferment, bottling, labeling, boxes). Very hands-on communication.

## Recommendation

**Go with Artisan Ferments Co.** Reasons:

1. **Fermentation expertise:** Non-negotiable. They understand 20°C control, temperature logging, salt ratios. Valley Food would treat this like vinegar-based hot sauce (different beast).

2. **Craft alignment:** Small operator, batch mentality, not commodity-focused. Mission fit is important for brand authenticity.

3. **Flexibility:** Can scale smoothly as demand grows (Valley's 3K minimum is a trap if demand is uncertain). Can rotate SKUs without hassle.

4. **Cost premium justified:** $0.25/unit (Artisan vs Valley) is acceptable given quality + flexibility. At $4.50 retail, that's 5.6% margin difference = manageable.

5. **Risk profile:** Established (not new), ferment-focused (not generic), transparent. Coastal Artisan is too risky year 1 (FDA oversight, startup brittleness).
