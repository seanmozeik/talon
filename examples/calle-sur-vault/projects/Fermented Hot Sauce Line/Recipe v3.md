# Recipe v3 — Fermented Hot Sauce (Final)

**Status:** Locked. Ready for co-packer handoff.

**Yield:** 500 ml bottles (per batch: 5 liters = ~10 bottles)

## Batch Size (5 Liters)

| Ingredient      | Weight  | % by wt | Notes                     |
| --------------- | ------- | ------- | ------------------------- |
| Red Fresno      | 1350g   | 60%     | Fresh or thawed frozen    |
| Habanero        | 565g    | 25%     | Fresh preferred           |
| Aji limo        | 340g    | 15%     | Fresh or frozen           |
| Kosher salt     | 64g     | 2.5%    | (60% peppers + 4% brine)  |
| Water           | 360 ml  | ambient | For brine phase           |

**Total pepper weight:** 2,255 g (wet, as-is)  
**Total ferment weight:** ~2,679 g (with salt + water brine)

## Technique: Lacto-Fermentation

### Prep (Day 0)

1. **Wash peppers** under cool running water. Pat dry.
2. **Weigh out:** 1,350g Fresno, 565g habanero, 340g aji limo. Keep separate (blend later).
3. **Stem & seed:** Remove cap and as many seeds as practical. **Do NOT remove all seeds**—some remain for texture & heat distribution. Yield: ~85% of original weight (waste acceptable).
4. **Coarse chop:** 2–3 cm chunks. Texture matters; don't puree yet.

### Ferment Setup (Day 0, afternoon)

1. **Brine:** Mix 360 ml water + 64g kosher salt. Stir until dissolved. Target: ~15% salinity by volume (shelf-stable brine for long ferments).
2. **Pack jar:** Use clean, non-iodized glass jars (1-liter capacity, fill 3–4 jars per 5L batch). Layer chopped peppers + brine in alternating fashion. Pour remaining brine to cover peppers by 1 inch.
3. **Weight:** Use glass weight or food-grade plastic bag filled with brine. Keep peppers submerged (mold risk if exposed to air).
4. **Cover:** Cloth + rubber band (breathable, prevents dust/bugs). **Not airtight** (CO₂ escape needed).
5. **Ferment location:** 18–22°C, away from direct light. Ideal: dark fermentation chamber or temperature-controlled cabinet.

### Fermentation Progression (Days 1–14)

**Daily observation (first 3 days):**
- Cloudy liquid = normal (wild microbes colonizing)
- Smell = pleasantly sour, funky, alive (not rotten)
- Mold on surface = remove immediately with clean spoon (rare if brined properly)
- Taste: Day 1 = salty, peppery. Day 2 = slight tang. Day 3 = noticeable ferment funk.

**Fermentation window:**
- **Day 5:** First real tanginess. Peppers soften. Tasting window opens.
- **Day 10:** Strong acidity. Peppers yield easily to pressure. This is "done" for most palates.
- **Day 14:** Mature ferment. Deep, complex funk. Peppers falling apart slightly. **Target harvest point.**

**Taste testing (from Day 5 onward):**
- Pull 1 tsp with clean spoon
- Taste: Looking for **bold acidity + deep pepper flavor**
- Texture: Peppers should yield but not mush (gentle squeeze = slight resistance)
- If mushy by Day 10: temperature likely too high. Taste and harvest immediately.

### Post-Ferment Processing (Day 14)

1. **Drain:** Separate peppers from brine. Reserve brine (will use for final consistency adjustment).
2. **Blend:** Fermented peppers → food processor or immersion blender. Ferment liquid addition (50 ml brine) to aid blending. Blend until smooth (no visible chunks). Let stand 5 min, re-blend if needed.
3. **Strain (optional):** For silky sauce, pass through fine sieve (removes seeds/skin particles). Yield loss: ~10%. For rustic texture, skip this step.
4. **Consistency adjustment:** Target: pourable but thick (like ketchup, not watery). Add reserved ferment liquid 30 ml at a time until desired thickness.
5. **Final taste:** Adjust salt if needed (usually unnecessary; ferment salt level is already balanced). Add black pepper if desired (0.5–1 tsp per 5L batch, optional).

## Iteration History

**v1 (Nov 2025):** 70% Fresno, 30% habanero, no aji limo. 2.5% salt, 10-day ferment. **Issue:** Too thin consistency, flavor one-note (habanero dominated). Heat too high for mainstream appeal (8/10). Shelf life concern (lower acid from shorter ferment).

**v2 (Jan 2026):** 65% Fresno, 20% habanero, 15% serrano. 2.5% salt, 12-day ferment. **Issue:** Serrano flavor was muddy, ferment was sharp (over-acidic). Revision: aji limo instead of serrano.

**v3 (March 2026 — locked):** 60% Fresno, 25% habanero, 15% aji limo. 2.5% salt, 14-day ferment. **Result:** Balanced heat (5/10), complex depth (Fresno backbone + habanero spice + aji limo brightness), silky texture, shelf-stable. Internal tasting approved March 28. Approved for co-packer handoff April 24.

## Safety & Shelf Life

- **pH:** Target <4.0 (acidic = safe, non-pathogenic). Measured on samples from v3: pH 3.4–3.6 (food-safe zone).
- **Salt concentration:** 2.5% = prevents pathogenic fermentation. Consistent across all batches.
- **Shelf life:** Unopened, room temperature, 18 months. Opened, refrigerated, 6 months.
- **Storage:** Avoid plastic (acidic ferments leach plasticizers). Glass bottles only.

## Scaling Notes for Co-Packer

- Temperature tolerance: 18–22°C is strict (outside range = flavor drift or safety risk)
- Fermentation vessel: Food-grade plastic or glass (no metal)
- Weight/Submersion: Critical—brine-submerged is non-negotiable
- Taste testing protocol: Daily sampling from Day 5 onward, documented
- Batch documentation: Ferment start date, temperature log, final pH, harvest date → archive with product lot
