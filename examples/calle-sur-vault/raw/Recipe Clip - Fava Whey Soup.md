# Fava & Whey Soup with Crispy Bread Crumbs

**Chef's Code** | Recipe by Thomas Lindberg

---

## Ingredients

- 2 lbs fresh fava beans (or 1 lb frozen, thawed)
- 3 cups fresh whey (from ricotta or chèvre production, or substitute vegetable stock)
- 1 medium onion, halved
- 4 cloves garlic, smashed
- 1 bay leaf
- 6–8 mint leaves, torn
- 2 tbsp butter
- Sea salt and white pepper
- 4 slices sourdough, cubed and toasted with olive oil and thyme

---

## Method

1. **Blanch and peel favas.** Bring a pot of salted water to boil. Drop in shelled fava beans (fresh from the pod) for 2 minutes. Drain, plunge into ice water. Pinch off the pale skin—the bright green interior is what you want.

2. **Build the soup.** In a large pot, melt butter over medium heat. Add halved onion and smashed garlic. Sauté until soft, about 5 minutes. Add whey, bay leaf, and blanched favas.

3. **Simmer gently.** Bring to a bare simmer. Cook for 15–20 minutes until favas are tender and the whey takes on a faint green cast from the beans.

4. **Blend.** Working in batches, blend soup until smooth but still slightly textured (don't over-blend into paste). Return to pot.

5. **Finish.** Stir in torn mint. Season with salt and white pepper to taste. The whey should taste both creamy and bright.

6. **Serve.** Ladle into warm bowls. Top with crispy bread crumbs, a drizzle of good olive oil, and a few whole mint leaves.

---

## Marco's Notes

> [!note]
> **I'd lacto the whey first.** If we're using whey from our own ricotta runs, ferment it 3–4 days with a bit of salt and a whey culture. Adds tang and complexity—less flat than raw whey. Also: the brightness of fava + fermented whey is a conversation.

> [!note]
> **Bread crumb garnish feels safe.** Try charred baguette shards instead, or fried capers. Something that adds bitterness to cut the richness.

> [!note]
> **Worth testing with spring garlic** (young, not cured). The sweetness would play differently against fermented whey.
