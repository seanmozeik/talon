# @sandorkraut: Koji Rice Fundamentals

**Instagram Post** | April 10, 2026

---

**Caption:**

After 20 years working with koji, the rice ratio stays the same. Simple. The mold knows what it needs. You're just the guardian.

**METHOD:**

- 1 kg sushi rice (white, medium grain—don't get fancy yet)
- 1-2 tbsp koji spores (koji-kin powder, or fresh koji chopped fine)
- Filtered water for steaming
- Cheesecloth

**PROCESS:**

1. Wash rice twice in cool water. Steam in a bamboo steamer or cheesecloth bundle until just cooked through but still firm. Temperature matters: aim for 27–30°C when you introduce the spores.

2. Spread rice thin on a baking sheet lined with cheesecloth. Sprinkle spores evenly across the surface. Fold the cheesecloth corners loosely—you want air, not a vacuum.

3. Incubate at 26–28°C in a humid environment (a cooler with a damp towel, or a kitchen cabinet with a temperature strip taped to the wall). Don't fuss. Don't open it constantly.

4. By day 2, white mycelium will start to creep across the rice. By day 4–5, the entire batch should be covered in white fuzz with a sweet, fruity aroma (like overripe melon).

5. When the koji smells fruity and sweet (NOT sour, NOT musty), it's ready. Dry it out on a screen in a warm, dry place for 2–3 days, then store in an airtight jar.

**VARIABLES:**

- **Temperature below 26°C?** Fermentation slows, mold competes with contaminants. Stay warm.
- **Humidity too low?** Koji dries out; spores fail to germinate.
- **Humidity too high?** You invite bacterial growth and off-flavors.

Hit the sweet spot and koji is foolproof.
