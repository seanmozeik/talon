# Pickling Brine Ratios

Quick-reference ratios for the brines we use most often. Anything below 2% salt
risks spoilage in warm prep; above 4% suppresses lacto fermentation. See
[[Lacto-Fermentation]] for the underlying biology and [[Salt Acid Fat Heat]]
for the seasoning theory.

## Salt-by-weight (always weigh, don't measure)

| Application | Salt %   | Notes                                                              |
|-------------|----------|--------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Quick pickle (3-7 days, fridge)         | 2.0%   | Cucumbers, radish, onion. Stay refrigerated.       |
| Lacto pickle (7-21 days, room temp)     | 2.5–3.0% | Kraut-style. See [[Lacto-Fermentation]] starter.  |
| Long ferment (>21 days)                 | 3.5%   | Olives, hot sauce mash, certain miso bases.        |
| Cure / charcuterie                      | 2.5–3% (with #1 nitrite) | Out of scope of this article — see meat program. |

## Vinegar pickles (non-fermented)

Standard sweet pickle: 1:1:1 (vinegar:water:sugar) with 1.5% salt by total
liquid weight. Use 5% acidity vinegar. Boil before pouring over packed jars.

## Common mistakes

- Topping off a fermenting brine with fresh water dilutes salt and invites
  yeast. Top off with a fresh 3% brine instead.
- Iodized salt suppresses lacto cultures. Use kosher or pickling salt only.
