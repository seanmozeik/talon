# Service Music Cues

Notes from the Calle Sur opening week sound design. Volume targets in dB(A)
measured at the host stand:

| Phase           | Time          | Target dB(A) | Tempo    | Vibe                              |
|-----------------|---------------|--------------|----------|-----------------------------------|
| Pre-service     | 5:30–6:00 PM  | 58           | 60–80    | Soft Spanish guitar, low texture  |
| Early service   | 6:00–7:30 PM  | 62           | 80–100   | Warm latin jazz, building         |
| Peak service    | 7:30–9:30 PM  | 66           | 100–120  | Energetic flamenco-fusion         |
| Late service    | 9:30–close    | 60           | 80–100   | Wind down, mellow                 |

Speakers tuned to attenuate above 4kHz so the room doesn't get fatiguing
during long tastings. The Bose system at the bar runs hotter (about +4 dB)
to mask blender noise during cocktail prep.

## Phase transitions

Crossfade over 60 seconds between phases. Avoid hard cuts — guests notice
silence more than they notice music. The host stand iPad runs the playlist
sequencer; manager has override on the back-of-house tablet.
