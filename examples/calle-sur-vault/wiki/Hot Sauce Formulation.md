# Hot Sauce Formulation

Hot sauce formulation is the reusable knowledge behind a finished bottle: pepper
selection, salt, fermentation time, acidity, texture, and packaging constraints.
It is not the same thing as the active retail project, which tracks launch
readiness, vendors, margin, and current production decisions.

## Formulation Variables

**Pepper mix.** Fresno gives red fruit and medium heat. Habanero gives aroma and
top-end heat. Aji limo gives brightness and a greener finish.

**Salt percentage.** Calle Sur uses 2.5% salt by pepper weight as the working
baseline. That level keeps the ferment moving without making the final sauce
taste flat or cured.

**Fermentation window.** Ten days gives a bright sauce. Fourteen days gives more
depth and funk. Twenty-one days works only for hotter or more aromatic peppers,
otherwise the batch can taste muddy.

**Acid shape.** The preferred line is lactic acid first, vinegar second. Vinegar
can sharpen the finish, but it should not become the main story.

**Texture.** Strained sauce is retail-friendly and clean. Rustic sauce reads more
kitchen-made but is harder for a co-packer to repeat.
