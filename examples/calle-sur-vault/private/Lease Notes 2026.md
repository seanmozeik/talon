# Lease Notes 2026

Current lease expires August 2027. Mr. Pereira (landlord) has been making moves. Initial conversation in February hinted at a renewal bump. Last week's call was less subtle: 18% rent increase. Market's tight on the coast; he knows Calle Sur is stable and profitable.

Running three scenarios:

**Scenario A: Accept 18% bump.** Rent climbs from $4,200 to $4,956/month. Adds ~$9,000 annually. Labor and COGS stay competitive; still margins work, but the tasting counter project becomes marginal. Food cost goes from 32% to ~33% to absorb the hit. Doable, but I lose the buffer for expansion projects.

**Scenario B: Negotiate to 12%.** Adds $504/month, $6,048 annually. Sustainable. Opens the door for [[Tasting Counter]] build-out in Q3 if Spring 2026 revenue targets hold. The build-out only makes sense if I can commit to 3+ years of stable rent. 12% is a hard floor with Pereira, but worth the conversation.

**Scenario C: Walk.** Find a new location. Unlikely — I'm rooted here, staff is here, the clientele is here. Two months of disruption, new landlord unknowns, buildout delays. Costs way more than an 18% bump. Not serious, but keeping it in the model.

> [!quote]
> "The lease is the foundation. Everything — the hot sauce line, the counter, even staying open — depends on getting this right. I need to know my rent ceiling before I commit to anything." — Marco, April 14

Decision deadline: June 2026. Need to know by then for [[Financial Projections 2026]].

Andrés (silent partner) is supportive but doesn't want to throw cash at the problem. Fair. The restaurant has to sustain itself. I'll negotiate hard on 12%, accept 15% if needed, and revisit Scenario C only if Pereira goes above 18%.

Next step: formal renewal conversation with Pereira week of May 5.
