# Financial Projections 2026

Three scenarios: base, hot sauce line, tasting counter. All assume [[Spring 2026 Menu]] baseline (covers ~$52 avg, 65 covers/night, 6 nights/week).

## Base Scenario (No New Ventures)

Keep operations as-is. Passive year.

| Metric | Q2 | Q3 | Q4 | Annual |
|--------|----|----|----|----|
| Revenue | $156k | $168k | $195k | $654k |

| Labor (34%) | $53k | $57k | $66k | $222k |
| Rent | $13k | $13k | $13k | $52k |

| Other (misc, insurance) | $8k | $8k | $8k | $32k |
| **EBITDA** | **$26k** | **$29k** | **$37k** | **$111k** |

Assumes Q4 bump (December holidays, tourist season), no major surprises. Margins are healthy but not expanding. Rent assumption: status quo ($4,200/month). See [[Lease Notes 2026]] for risk.

## Hot Sauce Line Scenario

Launch [[Fermented Hot Sauce Line]] in Q2 (soft launch), ramp through Q3–Q4. Co-packer handles production; Calle Sur handles bottling, labeling, distribution.

**Revenue add:** ~$8k Q2, $18k Q3, $28k Q4 (conservative; assumes 200 bottles/week at $15 MSRP, wholesale at $8). Total add: $54k annual.

**COGS add:** Higher margin than food (60% gross vs. 32% for kitchen). COGS on hot sauce line: 35% of revenue add.

**Labor add:** ~$2k/quarter (Renata's time on distribution, one line cook 4 hours/week bottling).

Hot sauce line adds ~$77k to annual EBITDA (higher because of better margins). Payoff timeline: breaks even on setup costs (bottler deposit, labels, initial ingredients) by Q3. Requires [[Co-Packer Research]] to be solid before I commit.

## Tasting Counter Scenario

Build and launch [[Tasting Counter]] in Q3 (August). 8 seats, ~$90/person, 4 nights/week. Assumes ramp to 6 covers/night by end of Q3, 8 covers/night by Q4.

**Revenue add:** ~$12k Q3, $28k Q4 (conservative; some cannibalization of dining room, but higher check average offsets). Also includes hot sauce line (both scenarios).

**COGS add:** Tasting menu is higher COGS than regular menu (~40% vs. 32%), but covers are higher.

**Labor add:** Dedicate Ana 20 hours/week to tasting menu (raise = ~$5k/quarter). One prep cook for counter mise.

**Rent impact:** See [[Lease Notes 2026]]. Tasting counter **only makes sense if lease renews at sub-12% bump.** If Pereira hits 15%+, the project doesn't pencil.

Tasting counter adds complexity (new menu, tighter seats, scheduling) but grows annual EBITDA to ~$186k. Only viable if lease is stable and spring revenue stays north of $52/cover.

## Decision Matrix

| Factor | Hot Sauce | Tasting Counter |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| Capital required | $8k | $35k+ |
| Execution risk | Low (co-packer handles mfg) | High (new service model) |
| Timeline | Q2 launch | Q3 launch (contingent on lease) |
| Staffing burden | Light (Renata + line) | Heavy (Ana dedicated) |
| Margin impact | Positive | Positive, if lease stable |
| Dependency | [[Co-Packer Research]] | [[Lease Notes 2026]] |

**Recommendation:** Launch hot sauce line Q2. Commit to tasting counter only after lease is signed at acceptable terms (12–15%). Both together, if conditions align, hits $764k annual revenue and stabilizes the business for growth in 2027.

Baseline (no new ventures) is safe; hot sauce is high-probability; tasting counter is contingent on landlord negotiations.
