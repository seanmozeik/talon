# Staff Issue April Conflict

Lúcia and Ana. Walk-in usage, prep timing, communication. Came to a head week of April 13. I should have caught the pattern earlier—I was slow.

## The Pattern

Mid-March: Ana starts her pastry prep around 6 a.m., before service begins. She uses one of the two walk-in stations for mise. Lúcia prepping sauce and stocks also at 6 a.m., needs the same station. They're bumping into each other, squeezing past, tension visible on the line.

By early April, they stopped saying "excuse me." Just grimace, aggressive shoulder work. Week of April 13, Ana left a tray of dough in Lúcia's spot. Lúcia moved it. Ana moved it back. By Thursday service, the air was poison.

## The Conversations

I talked to each separately on Friday April 18.

**Lúcia's side:** Ana shows up without a plan, just assuming the walk-in is hers. "I've been sous in three kitchens. No one else does prep at the same time in the same station." She's not wrong—it's messy choreography. She wants a schedule. Clear.

**Ana's side:** Ana felt locked out. She'd been starting early to get pastry mise done before dinner, but Lúcia was treating the walk-in like her own kingdom. "I'm not trying to be difficult, but I'm also not going to ask permission to do my job." Fair frustration.

Both respect each other. Neither was being unreasonable. They just needed structure.

## Resolution

**Shared prep schedule:** 6 a.m.–7 a.m., both are in the walk-in, but I drew a line down the middle—literally marked it with tape. Lúcia's left side (sauces, stocks), Ana's right side (pastry, dough). Each owns their zone. Sounds silly written down, but it works.

**Weekly 10-minute check-in:** Every Monday, 10 a.m., just me, Lúcia, and Ana. Five minutes to review the week ahead (specials, big prep runs), five minutes to flag any conflicts early. Prevents the same thing from festering.

**My role:** I'm checking in more. Walking the line during prep (6–7 a.m.), not managing from the office.

> [!quote]
> "I was slow to step in. Should have caught the pattern in March. Next time I notice tension on the line, I move on it immediately. That's on me." — Marco, April 18

## Outcome

By the following week (week of April 22), the tension was gone. They're still not best friends—they're professionals—but they're coordinated again. No more aggressive prep choreography.

Lesson: early detection. Watch the kitchen. Tension before it becomes conflict.
