## Costing Assumptions

1. **Ingredient costs:** Averaged from Q1 2026 supplier invoices (Costera Fish, Salt Marsh, Vega Dairy).
2. **Labor:** Plating + active cooking allocated at $15/hr (Lúcia's effective wage + overhead).
3. **Waste:** 8% ingredient waste factor applied across all vegetables.
4. **Fermentation:** Fava ferment labor + salt amortized across batch yield.
5. **Seasonality:** Spring prices (ramps, fiddleheads at peak). Summer & fall will shift (fava seasonal availability, lamb cost variability).

## Sensitivity Notes

- **Lamb price volatility:** $18–$22/lb range depending on sourcing. Our $18/lb is low; if Costera raises to $20/lb, Lamb Neck plate cost rises to $11.10 (margin drops to 67%). Mitigate: renegotiate contract or rotate protein.
- **Whey availability:** Fava Whey dish depends on fermentation yield. If ferment fails (rare), whey unavailable = dish offline. Keep 2-week frozen whey buffer.
- **Labor variance:** If plating time drifts (Lúcia training Jonas, slower output initially), margin tightens. Monitor plate times weekly.
